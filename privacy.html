Privacy Policy — Actually

Last updated: 29 June 2026

"Actually" ("the App", "we", "us") is a personal content-publishing tool used by its owner to upload their own short videos to their own social-media accounts (such as TikTok and YouTube). This policy explains what data the App handles.

1. Who this applies to

The App is operated by an individual creator for publishing their own content. It is not a public consumer service and does not create accounts for, or collect data from, third-party end users.

2. What data we access

  • TikTok account information — when you authorize the App, TikTok provides a user identifier (open ID), basic profile information, and an access token. These are used only to upload videos to your own authorized account.
  • Video files — the video files you choose are uploaded to TikTok on your behalf.

3. How we use it

Data is used solely to authenticate with TikTok and to upload your videos to your own account. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or any unrelated purpose.

4. Storage

Access tokens are stored locally on the operator's own computer (in a file named tiktok_token.json) only to avoid logging in repeatedly. Nothing is uploaded to any third-party server other than TikTok's own official API endpoints.

5. Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share any data with third parties. Data is exchanged only with TikTok's official API to perform the uploads you request.

6. Data retention & deletion

Locally stored tokens can be deleted at any time by removing the token file. You may also revoke the App's access from your TikTok account settings at any time.

7. Contact

For any questions about this policy, contact: marcturkesteen@gmail.com.
